Responsible Water Use

This section outlines how Summit Air minimizes water use in its Direct Air Capture and Storage operations while ensuring Wyoming’s water resources remain protected and available for future generations.

  • Low-Volume Consumption: Our DAC technology uses significantly less water than traditional oil and gas operations, helping to preserve local aquifers.
  • Closed-Loop Cooling Systems: Water is recycled and reused on-site to reduce withdrawals and eliminate discharge.
  • Groundwater Protection: All operations are designed to avoid impacts to drinking water sources, with monitoring aligned to EPA Class VI injection well standards.
  • Site Selection: Locations were chosen to avoid high-stress water basins and minimize ecological impact.
  • Regulatory Compliance: Full alignment with Wyoming DEQ and federal water permitting frameworks ensures responsible stewardship.

Wildlife Protection

This section explains how Summit Air minimizes its impact on Wyoming’s ecosystems by integrating wildlife protection into every phase of project planning and operations.

  • Low-Impact Siting: Project facilities are located to avoid critical habitats and migration corridors for native species such as pronghorn, mule deer, and sage-grouse.
  • Seasonal Timing Restrictions: Construction and maintenance activities are scheduled to minimize disruption during nesting, calving, and breeding periods.
  • Habitat Buffer Zones: Designated protective buffers are established around key wildlife areas, reducing noise and light pollution impacts.
  • Collaborative Planning: Summit Air works with local wildlife biologists, landowners, and agencies to guide responsible development.
  • Post-Construction Monitoring: Ongoing ecological surveys ensure that conservation goals are met and adaptive strategies are implemented as needed.

Secure CO₂ Storage

This section describes how Summit Air ensures the safe, permanent storage of captured CO₂ in deep geologic formations, using proven methods and rigorous oversight.

  • Class VI Well Compliance: Storage wells are designed and permitted under EPA Class VI regulations, with strict controls for long-term containment.
  • Geologic Suitability: Target formations are selected for their natural ability to trap CO₂, including thick impermeable caprock layers and deep saline aquifers.
  • Seismic and Subsurface Modeling: Advanced 3D geophysical modeling and simulation tools guide well placement and CO₂ plume tracking.
  • Monitoring, Reporting, and Verification (MRV): Real-time pressure sensors, seismic surveys, and groundwater testing provide continuous assurance of storage integrity.
  • Long-Term Stewardship: Summit Air works with state and federal agencies to ensure post-injection site care, including bonding and closure planning.

Summit Air’s CO₂ storage approach is built on decades of scientific research and industry experience, ensuring that every ton of carbon is stored safely, permanently, and responsibly beneath Wyoming’s resilient geology.

Low-Impact Pipeline Infrastructure

This section describes how Summit Air designs and installs CO₂ pipelines to minimize surface disturbance, protect land and wildlife, and ensure long-term operational safety.

  • Strategic Routing: Pipelines are co-located with existing roads and utility corridors to avoid greenfield development and reduce land fragmentation.
  • Trenchless Installation Techniques: Horizontal directional drilling (HDD) is used at water crossings and sensitive habitats to avoid surface disruption.
  • Right-of-Way Minimization: Narrow construction zones and fast reclamation timelines help limit impacts to rangelands and private property.
  • State and Federal Oversight: All pipelines are designed to meet or exceed PHMSA and Wyoming DEQ safety standards for CO₂ transport.
  • Community Engagement: Summit Air works closely with landowners, tribes, and local agencies to ensure transparency, access, and long-term maintenance plans.
