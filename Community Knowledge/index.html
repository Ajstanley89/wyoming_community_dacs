Community Knowledge & Education

Summit Air is committed to ensuring Wyoming communities are informed, involved, and empowered throughout every stage of our project.

Summit Air removes carbon while safeguarding Wyoming’s natural resources. Our approach includes careful water use, wildlife protection, secure CO₂ storage, and low-impact pipelines—built on science, compliance, and local responsibility.

Jobs & Opportunities

Summit Air’s project supports Wyoming’s workforce with real, local job opportunities—especially for those with experience in energy, construction, and the trades. From early-stage planning to long-term operations, our work creates stable, high-quality roles that align with Wyoming’s strengths.

1. Planning & Development Phase

  • Project Engineers: Focused on site layout, infrastructure design, and geotechnical planning—drawing on experience in oil & gas field development.
  • Permitting & Land Access Specialists: Professionals familiar with environmental reviews, leasing, and regulatory coordination.
  • Geologists & Subsurface Engineers: Experts who understand Wyoming’s rock formations and storage capacity, especially those with injection well background.

2. Construction Phase

  • Welders & Pipefitters: For pipeline installation and DAC system assembly—ideal for those from midstream and facility construction.
  • Heavy Equipment Operators: Skilled operators for earthwork, trenching, and site prep.
  • Electricians & Instrument Techs: Industrial wiring and control system installation, closely aligned with traditional energy project builds.
  • Safety Coordinators: HSE professionals with experience in high-hazard energy environments.

3. Long-Term Operations

  • Plant Operators: Overseeing DAC system performance, routine inspections, and carbon injection operations—comparable to gas plant or compressor station roles.
  • Maintenance Techs: Mechanical and electrical specialists for ongoing system support.
  • Environmental Technicians: Managing compliance monitoring, groundwater protection, and emissions tracking.
  • Logistics & Site Support: Fuel, parts, supply chain, and general services to keep the facility running efficiently.

Whether you’ve worked on drilling rigs, pipeline crews, or facility operations, your skills are needed here. Summit Air values Wyoming’s practical expertise and is committed to offering training, upskilling, and steady career paths in this new carbon management economy.

Training & Workforce Development

Summit Air is working with the University of Wyoming to create targeted training programs that prepare students and working professionals for careers in carbon removal, energy infrastructure, and environmental management.

Programs Designed for Wyoming’s Workforce

  • Skilled Trades Training: Programs for welders, pipefitters, electricians, and equipment operators—designed for workers with experience in oil, gas, and construction.
  • Engineering & Project Management: Coursework and applied learning in mechanical, civil, and energy systems engineering, including pathways for mid-career professionals transitioning from traditional energy fields.
  • Operations & Safety Certifications: Training for facility operators, safety coordinators, and maintenance technicians aligned with industrial standards and OSHA protocols.
  • Environmental Science & Monitoring: Academic and hands-on experience in air and water quality monitoring, subsurface assessment, and regulatory compliance—built for students interested in environmental roles tied to energy projects.

Pathways to Long-Term Careers

  • Internships & Capstone Projects: Summit Air offers project-based learning opportunities for University of Wyoming students across engineering, geology, environmental science, and policy.
  • Apprenticeships: Paid positions with partner contractors on Summit Air construction sites for students or career changers entering skilled trades.
  • Stackable Credentials: Certificate-to-degree pathways that allow workers to gain practical qualifications quickly while building toward longer-term education goals.

Whether you're coming from the oil patch, the classroom, or somewhere in between—these programs are built to support Wyoming residents who want to work in the next generation of energy and infrastructure.

Working with Wyoming Communities

Summit Air believes that strong partnerships lead to stronger projects. Our work is rooted in collaboration with local stakeholders, particularly the Northern Arapaho Tribe and the University of Wyoming—two key institutions shaping the state’s future.

Northern Arapaho Tribe

  • Early Consultation: Ongoing discussions with tribal leaders to understand local priorities, values, and cultural considerations related to land, water, and development.
  • Mutual Benefit: Exploring opportunities for long-term economic engagement, skills training, and infrastructure investment that support tribal interests.
  • Education & Transparency: Sharing project information in accessible formats, offering community briefings, and incorporating feedback into our development plans.

University of Wyoming

  • Research Collaboration: Partnering with UW’s engineering and geoscience departments on carbon storage, environmental monitoring, and DAC system optimization.
  • Local Workforce Pipeline: Creating internships, capstone projects, and career pathways for students in environmental science, energy engineering, and regulatory affairs.
  • Knowledge Sharing: Supporting public seminars, workshops, and educational resources to raise awareness around Wyoming’s role in carbon management innovation.

These partnerships help ensure our work reflects Wyoming’s values and creates long-term benefits for both local communities and the state’s energy future.
